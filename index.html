About Me

Hi I'm Myra Rudy! (Pronouced /ˈmɪrə/ or meer-uh) I am a student in the Year Up program taking courses through Seattle Central College.

I originally learned web development in middle school, but I haven't done much with it since high school. I'm currently re-learning and would love to have a fully developed site to display my work by the end of my program.

The photos shown here are photos of my girlfriend, taken by me. We met on a game in virtual reality called VRChat. Eventually I would like to have a page with my favorite photos that I can use as a sort of photography portfolio.

I was born in Utah where I lived until I was 8. My dad lost his life to leukemia in 2008, about a year before my mom finished college. My mom, my brother and I then moved to NYC as my mom obtained an internship there. We lived there for 11 years and I personally consider it the place I grew up in. While living there, my mom re-married and had two more sons, making me the eldest of 4. My step-dad obtained a job in Seattle early 2020 and was given resources to relocate which ended up taking 2 years because of COVID and the housing market. My oldest brother and I moved to Gig Harbor end of 2021 and lived with our grandparents until our parents were able to find a house.

I have way too many hobbies, many of which are very expensive. Skip the following paragraphs if you don't want to read me go ultra nerd mode about my hobbies.

I've been rock climbing since I was 11, around 5 years of my experience have been 3 days a week. I used to dream about becoming a professional climber but never ended up committing enough time into it.

I also love photography. I have been interested in photography for as long as I can remember. I always wanted to own a high end camera but I never owned one until about a year ago. My grandpa gave me a very cheap point and shoot camera which I used for a little while, but by the time I was in high school, most phones were already better than it. I first remember taking photos of my friends & I skateboarding. I ended up not taking skateboarding as seriously and just took photos of scenery. Then after moving to WA, I became more interested in cars and started taking photos of cars. Now that I've started Year Up, I have less time to go to car events, but mainly take photos of people or just anything of interest.

I also love basically anything related to physical hardwaare and physical computing. I remember messing around with my parents laptop when I was around 6 years old. Since then, I have always been involved with computers in one way or another. I built my first PC in high school and ended up buying and selling parts since. I consistenly change out my personal PC's parts so frequently that I don't think one part has stayed in it for more than a year.

My newest hobby has been collecting high end audio listening equipment. Mainly headphones, but I have been trying out some IEMs (In Ear Monitors) as well. I typically use a pair called from Sennheiser called the HD800 or a pair from Focal called the Radiance. A goal I have is to own a pair of Utopias from Focal. I had the opportunity to try a pair in NYC last time I was there and it ruinied my sense of hearing in an amazing way. I can't describe the experience in any other way than saying that, after I left, my AirPods sounded like a $5 pair of wired earbuds that you doused in water.
